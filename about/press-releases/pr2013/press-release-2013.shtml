Wednesday, June 5, 2013

Milestone Reached: 1 Million Pounds of Textiles Donated to re-fashioNYC, the City's Official Clothing Reuse and Recycling Program

Sanitation Commissioner John J. Doherty announced today New York City residents have donated and recycled more than one million pounds of clothing, shoes, accessories, towels, linens, and rags through the successful re-fashioNYC program. re-fashioNYC is a partnership between the Department of Sanitation and the nonprofit organization, Housing Works. It was developed to reduce the 200,000 tons of textile waste sent to landfills by New Yorkers each year. The program makes clothing donation and recycling as easy as possible by providing free donation bins, on-call pick up, tax receipts, and donation reports.
