No Garbage, Recycling Collection or Street Cleaning in Observance of Independence Day, Monday, July 5th

June 28,2010 | Vito A. Turso/Matthew LiPani

Press Release # 10-17, FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

The New York City Department of Sanitation (DSNY) announced today that in observance of Independence Day, there will be no garbage or recycling collection, or street cleaning on Monday, July 5th, 2010.  In addition, all Sanitation Department administrative offices will be closed on Monday, July 5th, in observance of the holiday.

Residents who normally receive Monday garbage collection should place their trash out at curbside Monday after 5 P.M. for pickup.  Those receiving Monday recycling collection will not have their recycling picked up until the following Monday, July 12th. 

For questions about Sanitation services and holiday schedules, contact 311 or visit the DSNY website at www.nyc.gov/sanitation.
