No Garbage, Recycling, Organics Collection or Street Cleaning on Memorial Day, May 26, 2014

May 21,2014 | Vito A. Turso/Belinda A. Mager

Press Release # 14-41, FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

Department of Sanitation (DSNY) announced today that in observance of Memorial Day, there will be no garbage, recycling or organics collection, or street cleaning on Monday, May 26, 2014.

 

  • Residents who normally receive Monday garbage and organics collection should place their trash and organic materials out at curbside after 5 p.m. on Monday, May 26 for pickup.
  • Residents receiving Monday recycling collection should place their items curbside, after 5 p.m, on Sunday, June 1 for pickup on Monday, June 2.

In addition, all Sanitation Department administrative offices will be closed on Monday, May 26, in observance of the holiday.

For questions about Sanitation services and holiday schedules, contact 311 or visit the DSNY website at www.nyc.gov/sanitation.

About the New York City Department of Sanitation

The Department of Sanitation (DSNY) promotes a healthy environment through the efficient management of solid waste and the development of environmentally sound long-range planning for handling refuse, including recyclables. The Department operates 59 district garages and manages a fleet of 2,022 rear-loading collection trucks, 450 mechanical brooms and 365 salt/sand spreaders. The Department clears litter, snow and ice from approximately 6,000 miles of City streets and removes debris from vacant lots as well as abandoned vehicles from City streets.
